Group expenses

Best app for group shared expenses (2026)

5 min read

Whether it's a trip, a shared flat, a wedding party, or a weekly dinner group — any time money moves between more than two people, someone ends up doing mental math on a napkin. A good group shared expenses app replaces that napkin with something instant, fair, and drama-free.

What to look for in a group expense app

  • Fast entry — typing, speaking, or scanning a receipt should take seconds, not a form with ten fields.
  • Flexible splits — not everyone shares every expense equally; a good app lets you assign costs to just the people involved.
  • Clear balances — you should always know, at a glance, who owes whom and how much.
  • Minimal settle-up — the app should suggest the fewest transfers needed to clear all debts, not a spreadsheet of every transaction.
  • No manual nagging — automatic, gentle reminders beat you having to text "hey, you still owe me for pizza."

Why Settlia stands out

  • AI receipt scanning — itemized, with tax and tip handled automatically.
  • Multiple payers per expense — because sometimes two people split the bill upfront.
  • Recurring expenses — rent, subscriptions, utilities added once, tracked every cycle.
  • Simplified or direct settle-up — pick minimal transfers, or pay exactly who fronted each expense.
  • Exportable reports — PDF/CSV for trips or shared households that need a paper trail.
  • Free to start, with optional Pro for unlimited groups and AI usage.

Who it's for

Roommates splitting rent and groceries every month. Friends on a trip splitting hotels, taxis, and dinners. Couples tracking shared costs. Any group where money changes hands and nobody wants to be "the accountant."

Mejor app para gastos de grupos compartidos (2026)

5 min de lectura

Ya sea un viaje, un piso compartido, una boda o una cena semanal entre amigos — cada vez que el dinero se mueve entre más de dos personas, alguien termina haciendo cuentas mentales en una servilleta. Una buena app para gastos de grupos compartidos reemplaza esa servilleta por algo instantáneo, justo y sin dramas.

Qué buscar en una app de gastos grupales

  • Registro rápido — escribir, hablar o escanear un ticket debería tomar segundos, no un formulario de diez campos.
  • Repartos flexibles — no todos comparten cada gasto por igual; una buena app te deja asignar costos solo a quien participó.
  • Saldos claros — deberías saber, de un vistazo, quién le debe a quién y cuánto.
  • Liquidación mínima — la app debería sugerir las menores transferencias posibles, no una hoja con cada transacción.
  • Sin perseguir a nadie — recordatorios automáticos y suaves valen más que tener que escribir "oye, todavía me debes la pizza."
